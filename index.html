Also called capital letters, caps, and upper-case, uppercase refers to letters in certain languages. In Latin script, the first three letters of the alphabet appear in uppercase letters are A, B, C, while in lowercase they appear as a, b, c. Another Western language that uses uppercase is Cyrillic. Some languages have no uppercase. Normally, if a letter has an uppercase, the letter has a corresponding lowercase. Grammar rules and conventions normally determine when to use uppercase though stylistic elements such a title can also determine when to use upper case.
